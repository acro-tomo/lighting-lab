Lighting decisions are often made from a plan and fixture schedule, before anyone can feel the room at night. LDK Lighting Lab gives homeowners a practical way to compare those choices in their own floor plan.

This sample project opens directly in the 2D editor. I select a living-room downlight, lower its brightness, and choose a warmer color temperature. The purpose is visual comparison, not certified lux calculation.

The fast Edit view keeps adjustments responsive. In Realistic mode, supported hardware progressively path traces the same scene so you can inspect both direct and indirect light.

When a direction is worth sharing, you can save a comparison and export a watermarked PNG. Projects autosave locally in the browser and can also be exported as JSON.

The interface switches between Japanese and English. On a phone, the workspace stays focused while the 2D and 3D views, settings, and editing actions remain available from compact controls.

For this Build Week release, I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to inspect the existing app, implement and verify the bilingual interface, harden feedback delivery, document the product, and validate the release. The existing editor and renderer were preserved.

LDK Lighting Lab helps people see and compare a home's nighttime atmosphere before construction makes changes expensive.
