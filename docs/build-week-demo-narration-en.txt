Choosing lighting for a new or renovated home usually starts with drawings and catalogs. For a homeowner, it is hard to imagine what the room will actually feel like. Will it feel bright enough? How much will warmer or cooler light change the atmosphere? Finding out after construction can make moving a fixture expensive.

Lighting Lab lets you explore those choices before construction. It runs in a browser. You can load your own floor plan, recreate the room, and try ideas without learning a complex CAD tool.

Here, I select a light. First, color temperature. I change it from twenty-seven hundred Kelvin to thirty-five hundred Kelvin. The room changes immediately.

Then I compare brightness. I move the light, and adjust the beam to make the light narrower or wider.

Each change appears in the two-D floor plan, so I can try another option quickly. In three-D, I can see how the lighting affects the furniture, walls, and room as a whole.

Finished Look adds indirect light for a more complete view.

Lighting Lab does not tell me there is one correct answer. It lets me compare options until the room feels right for the way I want to use it.

The two-D editor and three-D view stay synchronized. Projects can be saved and reopened in the browser, on a computer or a phone.

Codex with GPT five point six supported this project from the start. During Build Week, it reviewed lighting behavior and performance, and helped deliver the mobile experience, English interface, automated tests, and public release.
